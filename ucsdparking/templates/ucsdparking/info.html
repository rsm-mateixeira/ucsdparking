About

Welcome to our comprehensive project, a pivotal component of the Collecting and Analyze Large Data course at the University of California - San Diego, Rady School of Management. This initiative is not just an academic exercise; it's a practical solution tailored to enhance the daily experiences of our university community. By meticulously gathering and examining extensive parking lot inventory data, we aim to shed light on the fluctuating demands of park usage throughout the year. Our objective is to provide actionable insights that facilitate better planning and resource allocation for students and staff alike. Delve into our detailed analysis and discover how we're harnessing the power of big data to foster a more efficient and accommodating campus environment.

Data

After comprehensive research, our team has procured an extensive dataset detailing parking lot occupancy across the UCSD campuses in La Jolla and Hillcrest. This rich dataset encompasses a decade-long survey of parking spaces, capturing hourly occupancy details from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m., Monday through Thursday. Spanning from 2000 to the present, it offers an in-depth view of parking dynamics over the years.

For our analysis, we focused on data post-2018, providing us with a unique opportunity to examine the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on campus parking trends. Our approach involved dissecting the data by various categories including individual lots, structures, neighborhoods, areas, and specific locations. This granular analysis allows for a nuanced understanding of parking occupancy patterns during this unprecedented period. Importantly, the data we analyzed is based on one day of observation per parking lot per quarter, with observations taken every hour of that day. This approach ensures a comprehensive view of parking dynamics over different times and seasons.
