Use Case UCSD

A faculty member at UCSD plans to schedule a meeting with students and other staff members at the Geisel Library. They use the website to check the availability of parking spaces in lots close to the Rady School of Management. By viewing the dashboard, they can determine the best time to schedule the meeting, ensuring that all attendees will find parking conveniently. This tool also allows students and staff to plan their campus visits efficiently, selecting times when parking spaces are more readily available.

Use Case Rady School of Management

Parents visiting their child at UCSD for a special event at the Rady School of Management utilize the website to check parking lot availability. They choose the years and quarters from the options in the list, related to the date of their visit to view the average availability of the parking lots, parking spaces and at the hours of the day selected. This information helps them to plan their visit better, ensuring they arrive on time for the event. They got a permit of visitor from the permits website, so it's important to check the availability of this parking spaces.

Large Scale Use Cases

This tool would be well suited for integration with the UCSD parking services website, as it is it utilizes their interactive map to find specific parking lots available to students as well as information regarding their permit types, if UCSD Parking decided to integrate this tool they could directly filter by permit type based on availability to search for lots with more than X% of spots expected to be available at a given time to help individuals decide whether or not to purchase a parking pass for use at that time, or whether they would be better off finding other options for transportation.

Send to UCSD Campus planning for future development of parking based on current availability to address inefficiencies in parking allocation UCSD is a constantly expanding campus, even now new development is going on all throughout campus and extending its borders. As more and more students and faculty come to UCSD, more parking will be needed, and the school could use this information to find out which lots are currently struggling with providing adequate parking, as well as which lots are currently being under-utilized. With this information, they could take proactive steps to increase parking in the more heavily impacted areas of campus, as well as potentially use the area around the underutilized lots for other purposes, such as new buildings or dorms.
